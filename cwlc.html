Cold Weather Leader Course

The United States Army Cold Weather Leaders Course is the one and only place to earn the coveted Arctic Tab. Hosted by the 10th Special Forces Group, earners of the Arctic Tab are leaders and tacticians of the highest calibre, having proven that they are capable of surviving, navigating, and adapting under the harsh weather conditions of the Arctic and in being successful in conducting special operations within the most difficult course that the United States Army has to offer.

The Cold Weather Leaders Course consists of five parts with increasing difficulty where candidates will learn, and be tested on any and everything required to survive in the arctic climate. These five parts consist of the Aurora Phase, Lumi Phase, Neve Phase, Cold Weather Leaders Course Final Exam, and the Orion Phase (Graduation). Completors of the entirety of the Cold Weather Leaders Course will be immediately awarded the coveted Arctic Tab and are henceforth authorized to wear it on their uniforms.

Cold Weather Leaders Course Requirements

To attend the Cold Weather Leaders Course, one must be recommended by a superior after satisfying certain requirements.

General Requirements:

  • Maintain the rank of E4B, Corporal, or higher.
  • Served in current unit for at least one month.
  • No record of wrongdoing and a spotless record for the past 6 months.
  • Must be on PC with a working microphone.

Once a candidate meets these requirements, they are to be recommended by the following individuals:

Enlisted Recommendations:

  • E7 or lower → First E8B or Senior Enlisted in their chain of command or higher.
  • E8B → First E9A or Senior Enlisted in their chain of command or higher.
  • E9A → First E9B or Senior Enlisted in their chain of command or higher.
  • Divisional CSM → Command Level CSM or higher.
  • Command CSM → SMA or higher.

Commissioned Recommendations:

  • O1–O2 → First O3, Company Headquarters in their chain of command or higher.
  • O3–O4 → First O5, Battalion/Brigade HQ in their chain of command or higher.
  • O5–O6 → First O7, Regimental/Division HQ in their chain of command or higher.
  • O7–O8 → First O9, Command HQ or higher.
  • O9–O10 → Army Staff or higher.
