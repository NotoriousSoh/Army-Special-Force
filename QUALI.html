Qualification Badges

Here are all the badges that can be earned while serving in the Army Special Forces symbols of skill, dedication, and achievement. Each badge represents a milestone reached, whether through mastering advanced tactics, excelling in specialized training, or demonstrating exceptional performance in the field. These hard-earned insignias are more than just decorations; they are marks of honor, commitment, and the relentless pursuit of excellence that define every ASF operator.

Combat Infantryman Badge

The Combat Infantryman Badge (CIB) is a United States Army military decoration awarded to infantrymen who have engaged in active ground combat while serving as part of an infantry unit. It is a symbol of courage, skill, and dedication under the most demanding conditions of battle.

Eligibility: All members of the Army Special Forces are eligible to earn the CIB, with the exception of the Executive and Commanding Officers of ASF.

To earn the Combat Infantryman Badge, you must:

  • Serve as an infantryman during an approved deployment.
  • Actively participate in ground combat against another group recognized by the Quartermaster Corps.
  • Ensure the deployment is authorized by both ASF Command and Army Staff.

Earning the CIB is a testament to an infantryman’s direct combat experience and unwavering commitment to mission success.

Combat Medical Badge

The Combat Medical Badge (CMB) is a distinguished award of the United States Army, presented to members of the Army Medical Department who are assigned or attached to a ground combat arms unit of brigade size or smaller. Eligibility requires providing medical support during periods when the unit is actively engaged in ground combat.

Within the Army Special Forces, only members of the 5th Special Forces Group are eligible to earn the Combat Medical Badge.

To earn the CMB, you must:

  • Serve as a medic during an approved deployment.
  • Actively participate in ground combat against an opposing force recognized by the Quartermaster Corps.
  • Ensure the deployment has been authorized by both ASF Command and Army Staff.

The Combat Medical Badge honors medics who demonstrate courage and professionalism while delivering critical medical care under fire.

Combat Action Badge

The Combat Action Badge (CAB) is a United States military award granted to U.S. Army soldiers of any rank who are not assigned to infantry units, recognizing their active participation in combat. It honors those who are present and engaged with the enemy, performing their duties effectively and in accordance with established rules of engagement.

Within the Army Special Forces, eligibility for the CAB is limited to members of the High Command, including Executive and Commanding Officers.

To earn the Combat Action Badge, you must:

  • Serve in a non-infantry, non-medic role during an approved deployment.
  • Actively participate in combat against an opposing force acknowledged by the Quartermaster Corps.
  • Ensure the deployment is authorized by both ASF Command and Army Staff.

The CAB acknowledges the vital combat contributions of ASF leaders operating outside traditional infantry or medical roles.

Expert Infantryman Badge

The Expert Infantryman Badge (EIB) is a distinguished United States Army special skills badge awarded to infantry personnel who demonstrate exceptional proficiency in core infantry skills. Unlike the Combat Infantryman Badge (CIB), which is awarded for participation in active ground combat, the EIB is earned through the successful completion of the Expert Infantryman Badge Qualification Course—a rigorous program designed to test an infantryman’s mastery in combat operations, land navigation, and other mission-critical skills under demanding conditions.

Eligibility requirements to attend the EIB Qualification Course:

  • Hold the rank of [E3] Private First Class or higher.
  • Have Two-Factor Authentication enabled on both Roblox and Discord.
  • Use a PC for all course activities.
  • Possess a functioning microphone.
  • Maintain a clean record for the past month (no dishonorable discharges, suspensions, or arrests).
  • Have a minimum service period of 2 weeks.

All members of the Army Special Forces who meet these criteria are eligible for recommendation. Those who successfully complete the course will earn the Expert Infantryman Badge, a mark of exceptional combat readiness and infantry expertise.

Expert Field Medical Badge

The Expert Field Medical Badge (EFMB) is a prestigious United States Army special skills badge, first established on June 18, 1965. Serving as the non-combat counterpart to the Combat Medical Badge (CMB), it is awarded to U.S. military personnel who successfully complete a rigorous qualification process, including both written and performance-based evaluations. Within the ASF, earning the EFMB is a mark of exceptional medical proficiency and professionalism.

To earn this badge, you must:

  • Successfully complete an Expert Field Medical Course recognized and approved by QMC and Army Staff.

Eligibility requirements to attend EFMBC:

  • Be on a desktop or laptop device.
  • Serve in a medical unit.
  • Receive a recommendation from your direct superior.
  • Have a service period of at least 2 weeks.
  • Maintain a clean record for the past month, free from dishonorable discharges, suspensions, or arrests.
  • Hold a rank of [E3] Private First Class or higher.
  • Have Two-Factor Authentication enabled on both Roblox and Discord.

Note: Only personnel from the 5th Special Forces Group (5SFG) are eligible to apply for this badge.

Army Parachutist Badge

The Army Parachutist Badge is awarded to military personnel from any service branch who have successfully completed the U.S. Army Airborne Schools. This badge signifies that the wearer is a trained military parachutist, qualified to conduct airborne operations.

To earn the different levels of the Parachutist Badge, you must:

  • Basic Parachutist Badge: Complete a Special Forces Qualification Course recognized by QMC and Army Staff, and serve for at least 2 consecutive weeks in an airborne unit.
  • Senior Parachutist Badge: Complete the recognized Special Forces Qualification Course and serve for a minimum of 3 consecutive months within the same airborne unit.
  • Master Parachutist Badge: Complete the recognized Special Forces Qualification Course and serve for at least 6 consecutive months within the same airborne unit.

Each level reflects increasing experience and commitment to airborne operations within the ASF.

Army Air Assault Badge

The Air Assault Badge is a United States Army award granted upon successful completion of Air Assault School. It recognizes soldiers trained in air assault operations, including helicopter insertion, sling load operations, and rappelling techniques.

To earn the Air Assault Badge, you must:

  • Complete any Air Assault course recognized by QMC and Army Staff.
  • Serve for a minimum of 2 consecutive weeks in your unit.
  • Be recommended by your direct superior.
  • Be assigned to a USAR Division.
  • Hold the rank of E3 (Private First Class) or higher.
  • Have Two-Factor Authentication active on both Roblox and Discord.
  • Maintain a clean disciplinary record with no dishonorable discharges or suspensions in the past month.

The Air Assault Badge signifies proficiency in air mobility and assault tactics essential to ASF operations.

Special Operations Diver Badge

The Special Operations Diver Badge is a U.S. Army military badge awarded upon successful completion of the Combat Diver Qualification Course (CDQC). This badge recognizes specialized proficiency in combat diving operations, critical to underwater and maritime mission capabilities.

To earn the Special Operations Diver Badge, you must:

  • Complete a CDQC course recognized by QMC and Army Staff.

To earn the Special Operations Diving Supervisor Badge, you must:

  • Complete the recognized CDQC course.
  • Serve for 6 consecutive months in the same unit following course completion.

Eligibility requirements to attend CDQC:

  • Hold the rank of [E5] Sergeant or higher.
  • Use a desktop device (mobile devices are not permitted).
  • Have served in your division or unit for at least 1 month.
  • Possess a working microphone.
  • Be assigned to one of the following units: JFKSWCS, ASF, 75th Ranger Regiment, SOAR, 1st Infantry Division, 82nd Airborne Division, or 101st Bastogne Brigade.
  • Maintain a clean disciplinary record with no dishonorable discharges, suspensions, or arrests in the past month.
  • Be recommended by your direct superior.
  • Have Two-Factor Authentication enabled on both Roblox and Discord.

The Special Operations Diver Badge signifies elite underwater operational capability within the ASF and affiliated units.
