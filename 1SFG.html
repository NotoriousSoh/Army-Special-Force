1st Special Forces Group

The 1st Special Forces Group serves as the primary combative force of Army Special Forces, structured into two specialized companies: Alpha and Bravo. Alpha Company is the more elite company, tasked with operations centered towards First Person Shooters, participating in Scrimmages (Tournament Grounds) and Raids (Raid Grounds), where the precision, speed, and combative abilities of all operatives are crucial. Bravo Company on the other hand, also handles First Person Shooters, aiding and supporting Alpha Company.

Company Command of Alpha Company

Alpha Company is the ASF’s elite First-Person Shooter (FPS) unit, specializing in high-intensity engagements such as competitive scrimmages and precision raids. Known for their speed, accuracy, and tactical cohesion, Alpha Company consistently operates at the highest levels of performance. The company’s operations are spearheaded by Operational Detachment Alpha (ODA) 1111, a team of highly trained specialists capable of executing fast-paced, coordinated strikes in any virtual battlespace. Alpha Company stands at the forefront of ASF’s FPS dominance.

Company Command of Bravo Company

Bravo Company is a highly skilled First-Person Shooter (FPS) unit within the ASF, focused on competitive scrimmages and coordinated raid operations. While slightly less elite than Alpha Company, Bravo maintains a strong reputation for tactical proficiency, teamwork, and consistent performance in the field. The company is led by Operational Detachment Bravo (ODB) 1121, a dedicated team trained to execute precise and effective missions alongside their Alpha counterparts. Bravo Company plays a critical role in maintaining the ASF’s operational readiness and combat capability.
