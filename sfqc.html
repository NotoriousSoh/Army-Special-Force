Special Force Qualification Course

The Special Forces Qualification Course is offered to all members of the United States Army as an opportunity to become an official member of Army Special Forces. A challenging, physically, and mentally demanding course, the Special Forces Qualification Course is regarded as one of the most difficult entry qualification courses within the United States Army.
To enter the Special Forces Qualification Course, one may either pass an application (see below for applications) or attend a tryout for their desired Special Forces Group. This Course includes 5 phases in total, varying depending on your desired Special Forces Group.

1st Special Forces Group

  • C1: Combat Evaluation against Designated Fighters
  • C2: Raid Evaluation
  • C3: Classroom Session

5th Special Forces Group

  • C1: CQB Evaluation
  • C2: Land Navigation
  • C3: Roles and Tactics

10th Special Forces Group

  • C1: Combat Evaluation
  • C2: Map Introduction
  • C3: Classroom Session

Universal Courses

  • C4: Airborne Qualification
  • C5: Practice Inspection

Once all 5 courses are completed, candidates will graduate from the Special Forces Qualification Course and be awarded their Green Beret and Special Forces Tab after a month of servitude.
